In 2025, we welcomed the first Spring Festival after its official inclusion in the UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage list. An increasing number of foreign tourists visited China to celebrate the festival with us. According to the official statistics from the National Immigration Administration, during the Spring Festival holiday, the national border inspection authorities facilitated a total of 14.366 million inbound and outbound trips by Chinese and foreign travelers, marking a 6.3% increase compared to the same period last year.

Meanwhile, our analysis of tourism data from multiple provincial culture and tourism departments showed a year-on-year growth in inbound tourist numbers during the holiday season.

When "China Tours" meet festive atmosphere, international social media witness foreign travelers document and share their experiences of China's beauty during the Spring Festival. The records and evaluations also declare that a series of outstanding Chinese cultures related to the Spring Festival, a festival with a long history, have a place in the travels of foreign tourists.

We took "China Travel" and "Chinese new year" as the key words, and selected the short videos on tiktok that were posted around the Spring Festival and had high popularity. These clips, recorded by international travelers, showcase diverse festive celebrations across China - from lion dances and lantern festivals to dragon boat displays and calligraphy demonstrations - all proving particularly popular among foreign visitors.

Through word frequency analysis of English comments on these videos, we observed international netizens frequently using exclamations like "beautiful," "amazing," and "awesome" to praise cultural activities. Words like "love", "like" and "want" also demonstrate their fondness for Chinese cultural activities.

It is worth mentioning that words related to Chinese culture such as "culture", "dragon", and "lights" have also appeared in the comments many times.

Looking through the lens of the Spring Festival, but not limited to the period itself, we find that the cultural influence has long permeated "China travel".

The Chinese government has taken multiple measures to enhance the convenience of inbound tourism, which also gives in-depth "Chinese cultural Tours" more confidence to be chosen.

First, China's visa-free entry policies is continuously being upgraded.

Since January 2024, China has successively piloted unilateral visa-free policies for 38 countries including Switzerland, Australia, Portugal, Finland and South Korea.

On December 17, 2024, the National Immigration Administration announced a new visa-free transit policy, extending the stay time of visa-free transit foreigners in China from the original 72 hours and 144 hours to 240 hours (10 days). At the same time, the number of entry ports for visa-free transit personnel was increased to 60, and the area of stay and activity was expanded to 24 provinces.

On March 8, 2025, Brunei officially implemented a visa-free entry policy for Chinese citizens, achieving mutual visa exemption between China and Brunei. As of now, China has achieved mutual visa-free access with 26 countries.

In addition, in February 2025, a visa-free policy was issued for ASEAN countries' tourist groups to enter Xishuangbanna, Yunnan Province. The local customs and traditions of this city are also awaiting the arrival of foreign tourists.

We have conducted a statistical analysis of the continents where 26 countries offer mutual visa-free access, 38 countries offer unilateral visa-free access, and 54 countries offer visa-free transit, as well as the straight-line distances from China. By combining these policies, it can be found that China encourages long-distance countries to enjoy more abundant time and broader territory for inbound tourism in China. This makes in-depth "cultural tourism" more suitable for selection under such conditions.
